About Thomas Pluck

Blog About

I'm trying to figure out how networks learn—not just how to make them learn faster, but what's actually going on when a system updates itself. Somewhere between mathematics, neuroscience, and silicon.

Background: maths degree, MSc in Embedded AI, time in industry I don't miss. Now at GDSFactory. I've taped out chips, contributed to open-source EDA tools, and spent more time than is healthy thinking about sensitivity tensors and equilibrium propagation.

This blog is where I put the weird-ass ideas that don't fit the day job. Mostly: local learning rules on analog-friendly architectures, physical learning systems, equilibrium propagation in places it doesn't usually go, and whatever else I can't stop thinking about.
